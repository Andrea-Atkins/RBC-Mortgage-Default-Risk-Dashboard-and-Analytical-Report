# RBC-Mortgage-Default-Risk-Dashboard-and-Analytical-Report

**A single-bank, single-market credit risk dashboard tracking RBC's residential mortgage portfolio health across Ontario/GTA, Q2 2020–Q4 2025.**



## Project Overview

This project analyzes credit risk trends in Royal Bank of Canada's residential mortgage book, benchmarked against non-bank lenders and national/provincial arrears data. 

**The core question:** Is RBC's mortgage portfolio getting riskier, and if so, why — and what should the bank do about it?

## Key Findings

- RBC's mortgage impairment rate has risen for **9 consecutive quarters**, from a low of 0.09% (Q3 2022) to 0.37% (Q4 2025).
- RBC's impairment rate **crossed above** the non-bank lender arrears rate in early 2024 for the first time in the dataset — a reversal of its historical risk advantage. The gap stood at **0.11 percentage points** as of Q4 2025.
- Just over **half of RBC's mortgage book (50.8%)** is scheduled to renew within the next two years, much of it originated when interest rates were near zero.
- Ontario's arrears rate has risen faster than the national average since 2023, closing a gap that had persisted since 2020.

## Data Sources

| Source | Data Used |
|---|---|
| [RBC Quarterly Supplementary Financial Information](https://www.rbc.com/investor-relations/) | Mortgage balances, impairment rates, impaired loans |
| [Bank of Canada Financial Stability Indicators](https://www.bankofcanada.ca/) | Policy interest rate |
| [CMHC Residential Mortgage Industry Data Dashboard](https://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca/) | Non-bank lender arrears, renewal exposure benchmarks |
| [Canadian Bankers Association](https://cba.ca/) | Provincial (Ontario) and national arrears rates |

## Tools & Kills Used

- **Excel** — data staging, cleaning, and quarter-over-quarter calculations
- **Tableau Public** — dashboard design and interactive visualization
- **Analysis** — trend analysis, peer benchmarking, lagged correlation between policy rates and impairment
